The Collective

Built by sisters.
Grown by women.
Worn by everyone.

Margaret and Katherine Kleveland grew up in the coastal foothills of California, where the light has a particular quality and the summers feel unhurried. They looked at the fashion industry and found something missing: a brand that spoke to women the way women actually speak to each other.

So they built one. Not alone. DOEN was founded as a collective, with equity shared among the women who built it from the first day. That decision shaped everything that followed: who we work with, what we make, how we grow.

Copywriter's Notes
What changed and why

The Hero

The live DOEN homepage hero says one thing: "Summer Chapter One." Beautiful as a label. But a first-time visitor who does not already know DOEN lands on a chapter title without context for the story. This rewrite gives the hero a voice that matches the depth of the brand. "Made for the life you are already living in" is not a tagline. It is an invitation that assumes the woman already belongs here.

The Philosophy Strip

DOEN's values are extraordinary. Equity ownership, women-run supply chains, a B Corp certification earned in 2026. None of this lives anywhere near the homepage. The philosophy strip brings three core ideas forward without turning them into a manifesto. Short, declarative, memorable. The kind of copy a customer reads once and carries with them.

The Origin Story

"DOEN exists to honor the bond between women" is the most powerful sentence on the entire DOEN website. It currently sits near the bottom of the Our Story page, which most visitors never reach. This rewrite brings the sisters and their founding decision forward to the homepage, where it can do its actual job: convert a curious visitor into someone who cares about the brand before she cares about the price.

The Newsletter Capture

"Stay in touch. Keep up to date on the latest happenings in our world." That is the current signup copy. For a brand rooted in nostalgia, intimacy, and the particular feeling of coastal California womanhood, "latest happenings" is a significant mismatch. The rewrite positions the newsletter as access to something worth being inside. "The first to know is always inside" creates belonging rather than obligation. "Join the Collective" as the CTA is a direct echo of how DOEN describes its own founding team.

Before and After: Newsletter Capture
Live copy

"Stay in Touch. Keep up to date on the latest happenings in our world, launches, updates to our Impact Report, and more, when you subscribe."

Reads like a software company update digest. No brand voice, no sense of intimacy.
